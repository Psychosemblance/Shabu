For Shabu.

Distance measures geography. Not significance.

Shabu in my arms.

The Beginning

The first photo is always special ❤️

Not because it was flawless. But because it marked the moment where possibility became real.

Bloom

My Shabu — a phool ki kali.

Not loud. Not ornamental. Just quietly unfolding in her own certainty.

Conversation

I could listen to you all day.

Because when you speak, I feel included in your world — not impressed, not overwhelmed — simply connected.

Reflection

In every reflection, I see you.

Not just in mirrors. But in decisions, in thoughts I revisit, in futures I imagine.

Home

The only thing you love more than me… is food. Good. I’ll cook for you.

Shared meals. Quiet evenings. A life that doesn’t need spectacle.

Under the Stars

To that most memorable night under the stars.

Just quiet light, shared silence, and the kind of presence that makes the world feel smaller — and us feel certain.

The Future

I don’t want grand declarations. I want mornings. Conversations that run longer than planned. Familiar silences.

One day this page will feel unnecessary. Because I’ll say these things beside you — not across cities.

A Small Experiment

If you miss me, press the button.

Empirical conclusion: attachment persists across distance. Also — I miss you more.

I am building a life where you are not distant.
